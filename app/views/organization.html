Board of Directors

Andrew Hall

Chair

Andrew is an Assistant Professor of Biomedical Engineering at Saint Louis University, and a life-long resident of the St. Louis area. His career has included management positions with local biotech start-up Stererotaxis Inc., and most recently with Siemens Medical Solutions, where he was Director of Angiography Research Collaborations for North America. Born in Granite City, he attended Southern Illinois University in Carbondale for his undergraduate studies, and later earned his masters and doctoral degrees at Washington University. Andrew and his wife Ellen are the parents of a special needs daughter who is an ITNStCharles client. As such, they are particularly sensitive to the transportation needs of the disabled.

Randy Adler

Board Member

Randy Adler is a lifelong resident of the St. Louis area and grew up in the Alton, Illinois area. Randy is currently the Senior Logistics Specialist with the National Multiple Sclerosis Society in St. Louis, where he has served as the production lead on some of the country’s largest fund-raising events. Prior to that, he spent more than 20 years in operations management with retail companies such as Grandpa's and Dillard’s Department stores.

Randy and his wife Darlena reside in St. Peters, MO where he has been a driver for ITN since 2013. Prior to moving to St. Peters in 2013, Randy lived in Florissant, MO where he served on the Disability Awareness Commission. They have 3 children and 9 grandchildren and also serve as foster parents for the Humane Society of Missouri. Randy's father was diagnosed with MS at the age of 65 and because of this; he was able to see how the lack of mobility affected not only his father, but the entire family. Randy enjoys all the clients of ITN and believes that the service that is provided makes a difference in the quality of their lives. Randy's favorite saying is that “Every day is a good day that ends with the letter Y.”

Mark Crank

Secretary/Treasurer

Mark Crank founded Marcos Productions Inc. in 1980. Marcos Productions provides big screen video games, inflatable games, and variety performers to venues throughout the U.S. Mark is involved with many charities and was named O'Fallon MO Chamber Person of the year in 2011. He received awards from United Cerebral Palsy Association in 1985 and 1986, St. Louis Co. Juvenile Courts in 1990, and Who's Who in Business & Executives in 2001 and 2015. He has 5 children and 1 Grandchild. Mark's motto is "make life fun".

Jeff Hitchcock

Vice-Chair

Jeffrey Hitchcock is retired which enables him to spend his time volunteering and participate actively in his church. Jeffrey retired in 2013 after spending 12 years as a Regional Sales Manager for a global data center managed services firm. Prior to that, he spent 13 years with an international consulting firm in a number of capacities including St Louis branch manager. Jeffrey and his wife Karen reside in St Charles, MO. They have two married children and 4 grandchildren.

Jeffrey enjoys working with seniors and people with special needs and sees the importance of the transportation services that ITN provides in each of their lives. Every person that rides with ITN has a wonderful life story and it is an honor to be a part of their lives if only for a few short moments.

Sharon Barnhart

Board Member

Sharon Barnhart has dedicated her entire professional career to helping seniors and their families. She has over 19 years of experience of providing resources for seniors. Her career began as a social worker serving in long term care communities. In 2012, Sharon helped open a new Assisted Living and Memory Care community in Ellisville, MO. Currently, she is serving as an Elder Care Advisor for Americare working with three different Assisted Living and Memory Care communities. Sharon was introduced to ITNGateway by its founder, Althea West, and joined the board in the fall of 2017.

One of the things Sharon enjoys most about volunteering with ITN is all interesting clients she meets. Every client has a story and many of them don't have anyone to share it with. She believes the service that ITN offers their customers is invaluable and has improved their overall quality of life. Sharon, her husband, Chuck, and their energetic son, Christian moved to St. Peters seven years ago.

Donna Beck

Board Member

Donna is an Entrepreneur and has founded three small businesses, one of which continues today through affiliation with Brentwood Travel. She belongs to a select group of travel professionals achieving the highest industry certification, Certified Travel Counselor and is near completion of certification as a Master Cruise Counselor. In 2016, Donna was awarded the ASTA (American Society of Travel Agents) and TourismCares Rigby Healy Simmons Fellow Award and Scholarship. In 2017, she received one of eleven Top Contributor in Travel & Tourism awards given in the United States and Canada from Alignable, a professional online social media organization.

She received her BS in Marketing Management with minors in Communication and English from Southeast Missouri State University and has used her education and life training becoming actively involved in groups and organizations within the St Louis Metro community. In 2010 she received the Chesterfield Chamber of Commerce Volunteer of the Year Award for years of service and for founding and mentoring the Chesterfield Chamber Young Professionals.

Donna has been an Elected Board Member of the Mid-East Area Agency on Aging, on the Leadership Advisory Committee of Paraquad and devotes special time and effort raising awareness of Dementia, especially Lewy Body Dementia, while advocating for Seniors aging in place and for ensuring their safety and well being in the community. She is an Election Supervisor and works with OneCause weekends as an Assistant Event Manager at local charitable events of Not For Profit organizations.

Caregiving for her husband and her mother 24/7 until their passing, she understands the importance of social interaction and the ability to have services and transportation for Seniors to ensure an environment of possibilities and choices to remain active, involved and not separated and isolated.

Staff

Susan Kallash-Bailey

President/Executive Director

Susan Kallash-Bailey has been the Executive Director for ITNStCharles (Independent Transportation Network) since the fall of 2011. Her career in the travel and event business covered 28 years; the last 18 with Maritz, Inc. Having held a variety of roles, Susan served more recently as Vice President, Interactive Technology. Her passion and strength were visible as she collaborated with service providers to assess, evaluate, develop, and deliver solutions to improve the quality of work life for business customers and employees. Prior to joining ITN, Susan and her husband Ken operated an auto transport business; so transportation is part of her life blood. As helping others is, also, part of the fabric of her life, she has embraced the mission of ITNStCharles, and is dedicated to and passionate about making a difference in the lives of older adults and adults with disabilities who are unable to drive. Susan and her husband Ken of 28 years are loving a new phase of their life - becoming grandparents.

Start an ITN in your Community

ITNGateway is part of the ITNAmerica family, which unites organizations across the country working to support the transportation needs of older people. The ITNAmerica model offers a platform for dignified, sustainable mobility using technological solutions and innovative programming.
